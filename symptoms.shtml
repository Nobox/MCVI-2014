Know the warning signs.

Listen to your heart. And your body. If you have any questions or concerns about the physical signs of cardiac health issues, we encourage you to use this guide as a quick point of reference only. It’s not a diagnosis, but rather a source of information about some of the more common symptoms that can be associated with heart issues.

IF YOU BELIEVE YOU’RE EXPERIENCING A SERIOUS MEDICAL ISSUE, DO NOT DELAY, CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY.

We’re in this together, and we care about your heart and health. Be informed and prepare yourself before you experience any of these symptoms.

Chest pain is a leading indicator of potential heart issues. Chest pain is any kind of discomfort or pain that can be felt anywhere along the front of your body, as well as around your neck and upper abdomen. The most common types of chest pain range from tightness to pressure and general discomfort. While chest pain can be caused by a number of factors and many have nothing to do with cardiac issues, you should not take any risks if you experience it. In worst-case scenarios, chest pain can be an immediate precursor to a serious heart issue, such as a heart attack or stroke.

While shortness of breath has many different causes, it’s also associated with heart issues. These issues include heart attacks, congenital heart disease, heart rhythm disturbances and heart failure. When these conditions cause shortness of breath, it’s often because your heart is unable to provide enough oxygen to body parts such as your brain, muscles or other body organs.

When it feels like your heart is pounding or racing, those sensations or feelings are referred to as palpitations. Normally palpitations are not serious and can be the result of a number of everyday causes, ranging from exercise to caffeine. On occasion, however, they can be a sign of abnormal heart rhythm brought about by issues affecting the heart.

Cold extremities occur when circulation issues, including constricted or slightly obstructed blood vessels, cause a reduction of blood passage in the skin. In extreme cases, this feeling may be accompanied by changes in skin color and a throbbing or buzzing feeling. While there are many different non-cardiac reasons for the feeling of cool extremities, they can be an indicator of underlying heart issues, such as coronary heart disease.

more

Numb extremities, as well as a tingling feeling in those same areas, are characterized by abnormal losses of feeling, most often in the hands, arms, legs and feet. There are a host of different reasons why you could be experiencing numb extremities. If a heart issue causes it, the most common reason is due to a hardening of the arteries, which leads to a lack of proper blood flow to these areas. This can be an early sign of greater future heart problems, including a stroke.

more

High blood sugar levels create an elevated risk of heart issues for anyone experiencing them over long periods of time, whether they have diabetes or not. When left untreated, high blood sugar can slowly damage blood vessels throughout the body, leading to a host of major potential heart complications. For people with diabetes, the risk of heart disease is nearly twice that of a person without diabetes.

Vision changes such as blurred vision, seeing “halos” and having blind spots can be caused by a large number of conditions. They can also be indicators of underlying heart issues. These potential heart issues include strokes, blood loss or restriction of blood flow to parts of the brain and an inflammation of an artery in the brain that supplies blood to the eyes.
