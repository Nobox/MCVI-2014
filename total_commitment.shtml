Cardiomyopathy/Heart Failure

When your heart muscle measurably weakens or deteriorates for any reason, this is referred to as cardiomyopathy, which literally means “heart muscle disease.” This leads to the heart being unable to properly pump, which then leads to a host of heart conditions, including heart failure. Our specialized, multidisciplinary team uses sophisticated diagnostic equipment and facilities to immediately treat all types of heart failure.

Electrophysiology

The specialized methods of diagnosing and treating severe, troublesome cardiac arrhythmias (irregularities of heart rhythm) are at the heart of cardiac electrophysiology, a subspecialty of cardiology. These treatments are for those cases that can’t be managed with medication and for patients who can’t tolerate severe medications. We utilize ablation techniques, device-based therapy and sophisticated intracardiac mapping techniques to deliver this care. Additionally, our cardiac electrophysiologists work closely with each patient's clinical cardiologist and/or primary care physician, and are skilled at choosing the appropriate arrhythmia treatment for each individual patient.
